Joan Branca

Joan Branca paints the landscape she is most intimate with. Along Long Island's north shore, Ms. Branca fixes in paint her experience of a sense of place. Weaving a tapestry of color and light, Ms. Branca invites the viewer into the canvas to observe the rhythms and atmosphere of coastal scenes. The artist works both “en plein air” and in her Long Island studio, creating vibrant oil paintings that are a captivating expression of the beauty and mystery of nature in all seasons.

"My paintings are my response to the land," states Ms. Branca, "recollections of elemental things filtered through memory: some angle of land meeting water altered only by the tides, the quality of light as rock meets shore, the changing sky."

An alumna of the University of Pennsylvania Graduate School of Fine Arts, Ms. Branca solidified her commmitment to art after studies at the State University of New York at Cortland and the Skowhegan School of Painting and Sculpture in Maine. Neil Welliver, Janet Fish, Paul Georges and Yvonne Jacquette, among others, served as mentors throughout her artistic studies.

Ms. Branca’s artwork is represented in private, corporate and public collections, among them the Herbert F. Johnson Museum of Cornell University, The Nassau County Museum of Fine Arts on Long Island, The Munson Williams Proctor Museum, and the Foundation for Latin American Anthropological Research in Guatemala.

Her paintings have been included in several regional exhibitions throughout the mid-Atlantic, including the Arnot Art Museum, The Heckscher Museum and the Islip Museum. She has received the prestigious Painting Fellowship from the New York Foundation on the Arts, two Artist-in Residence grants from the New York State Council on the Arts, the Islip Art Museum First Place Award for Works on Paper, and the Heckscher Museum of Art Award for Painting. Ms. Branca currently exhibits with Gallery North on Long Island’s North Shore.
